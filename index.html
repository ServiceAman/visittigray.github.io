Discover Tigray

Welcome to the land of ancient wonders and vibrant culture! Tigray, nestled in northern Ethiopia, beckons history buffs, adventurers, and curious minds to explore its rich tapestry of heritage and landscapes.

Unravel the mysteries of the Aksumite kingdom, traverse the rugged terrain of the Tekeze and Gash rivers, and immerse yourself in the warmth of Tigrayan traditions.

From the legendary Aksum obelisks to the bustling markets of Adwa, Tigray is a treasure trove waiting to be uncovered. Join us on a journey through time and culture in this captivating region.

The Church of Our Lady Mary of Zion is a historically significant church located in Aksum, in the Tigray region of northern Ethiopia. It is one of the most important religious sites for the Ethiopian Orthodox Church and holds a central place in Ethiopian Christianity.

Medhane Alem Adi Kesho is located in the Tigray region of northern Ethiopia. It is known for its ancient rock-hewn churches, similar to those found in Lalibela.

Curious Minds Ask

What makes Tigray unique?

Tigray's blend of history, culture, and natural beauty sets it apart from the rest.

Best time to visit Tigray?

The ideal time to visit Tigray is during the dry season from October to February.

Is Tigray safe for tourists?

Tigray is a safe destination for tourists, with friendly locals and welcoming communities.

How to get to Tigray?

Fly into Mekele Airport and explore Tigray's wonders through various transportation options.

What to pack for Tigray?

Pack light, comfortable clothing, sturdy shoes for hiking, and a sense of adventure!
